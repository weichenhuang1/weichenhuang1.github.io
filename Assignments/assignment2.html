When dealing with missing values, it is important to recognize the frequency and context of the missing values. If the frequency is very low (few percent), then we still have enough of the data to work with that would produce a result that is representative of the sample. However, if the frequency is higher (10% or greater), then we should look into the reason for the response and why the question or survey could lead to the lack of a response. In other works the lack of response can also be interpreted as a response and we can look into the environmental variables that contribute to this response.

To explore the relationship between Tondu and other variables, I would plot the Tondu sentiment distributions in each of the groups, and see if the distribution varies significantly between each of the variables. In addition, I can run a t-test and determine if Tondu is dependent on any of these variables.
