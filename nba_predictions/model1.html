Classification Model 1

Author

Wei-chen Huang

Overview

This is a machine learning model generated using the Keras and TensorFlow libraries in python. This model was trained using the offensive efficiency, defensive efficiency, effective field goal percentage, and rebound differential of every team and the outcome of every game in the 2022-2023 NBA season. Given an input of two teams, it will print the predicted likelihood if each team winning the game.

Results

After training the model for 200 epochs, it was able to predict the correct outcome around 60-65% of the time, which in my humble opinion, not bad given the many limitations of the model that I address in the next section.

In addition, it seems to accurately predict blowouts, or when a team has a very large chance to win. Here, it predicted that the Milwaukee Bucks, one of the best teams in the league, has a 90% chance to defeat the Detroit Pistons, one of the worst teams in the league.

Limitations

I am aware that many key variables that can impact the outcome of an NBA game are not present in this first model. In the future, I plan on including home/away with home court advantage statistics for each team, a track of the active players for each game to account for injuries (tracked by an aggregate statistic like real plus-minus or 538’s RAPTOR, or my own aggregate statistic, and clutch statistics for each team. In addition, I will account for trades in future models, again with an aggregate player statistic.

I also believe that a classification model is not the most appropriate for NBA predictions, as the margin of victory is not given to the model to train. Therefore, it discrepancy between good teams and mediocre teams in diminished in this model. In my opinion, a linear regression model would produce better results here.
